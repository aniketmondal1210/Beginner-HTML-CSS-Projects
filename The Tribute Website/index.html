Mahatma Gandhi

1869 - 1948

Father of the Nation

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi ( October 2, 1869 â€“ January 30, 1948 ) A key figure in India's fight for independence from British rule, Gandhi pioneered the philosophy of nonviolent resistance, or Satyagraha. His commitment to ahimsa (nonviolence) and truth shaped the course of history. Gandhi's activism began in South Africa, where he fought against racial discrimination, laying the foundation for his later endeavors in India. He returned to India in 1915 and became a prominent leader in the Indian National Congress. Gandhi's leadership was marked by iconic movements, including the Salt March in 1930, a protest against the British salt monopoly. He advocated for self-reliance, communal harmony, and equality. The Quit India Movement in 1942, urging an end to British rule, showcased his unwavering commitment to independence. Tragically, Gandhi's life was cut short when he was assassinated on January 30, 1948, by Nathuram Godse. Despite his death, his principles left an indelible mark on the world. His philosophy of nonviolence influenced civil rights movements globally, including Martin Luther King Jr. in the United States. Gandhi's legacy extends beyond politics, emphasizing simplicity, truth, and the pursuit of justice. He remains an enduring symbol of peaceful resistance, and his birthday, October 2, is celebrated as the International Day of Non-Violence. Mahatma Gandhi's impact on the world is immeasurable, his life embodying the transformative power of nonviolence in the face of oppression. His teachings continue to inspire individuals and movements worldwide, standing as a testament to the enduring force of peaceful activism.

Biographies

  • "The Story of My Experiments with Truth" (Autobiography of Mahatma Gandhi): Gandhi's autobiography, written in Gujarati and later translated into English, provides a firsthand account of his life, thoughts, and evolution of his principles.
  • "Gandhi: An Autobiography" by Louis Fischer: This biography delves into Gandhi's life, exploring his thoughts, struggles, and impact on the world. Fischer provides insights into Gandhi's philosophy of nonviolence.
  • "Gandhi Before India" by Ramachandra Guha: Guha's comprehensive biography focuses on Gandhi's early life and experiences, providing context to the development of his political and moral philosophy.
  • "Great Soul: Mahatma Gandhi and His Struggle with India" by Joseph Lelyveld: This biography explores Gandhi's life, especially his years in South Africa and his relationship with India. It delves into his complex personality and relationships.
  • "Gandhi: His Life and Message for the World" by Louis Fischer: Another work by Fischer, this biography offers an overview of Gandhi's life, emphasizing his teachings and their relevance to the global community.
  • "Gandhi: The Years That Changed the World" by Ramachandra Guha: This is the second volume of Guha's two-part biography, providing an in-depth look at Gandhi's life from 1914 to 1948.
  • "Gandhi: A Political and Spiritual Life" by Kathryn Tidrick: Tidrick's biography explores Gandhi's life from a political and spiritual perspective, shedding light on his interactions with other leaders and the development of his philosophy.
